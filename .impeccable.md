## Design Context

### Users
Carter Wang's personal website is primarily for people who want to understand Carter quickly: founders, investors, collaborators, readers, friends, and anyone evaluating his work, writing, projects, and life context. The job is to create a memorable first impression, then make it easy to move into a topic-specific page without feeling like a generic resume site.

### Brand Personality
Immersive, thoughtful, ambitious. The experience should feel personal and transportive, with a sense of entering different worlds as the user scrolls. It should avoid startup-template polish, tacky sci-fi motifs, fake logos, generic CTA clutter, and copy Carter did not write.

### Aesthetic Direction
A refined cosmic personal site: Carter's name appears in a galaxy/space environment, then scrolling reveals distinct worlds for Work, Writing, Projects, and Life. Visuals should be clear, consistent, not overly complex, and should feel like the page exists in space rather than showing isolated pictures of space.

### Design Principles
- Make the landing page feel like an environment, not a poster or marketing page.
- Keep personal-site hierarchy restrained: navigation and social links should be useful but secondary.
- Each topic page should inherit the same cosmic system while feeling specific to its topic.
- Use only real, useful copy and avoid invented slogans or filler.
- Prefer crisp, simple visuals and purposeful motion over busy effects.
